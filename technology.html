Artificial Brain

Our artificial brain is able to perceive the observable surrounding environment, reason about the hidden states, and react properly to serve its goal and intention.

show more

Perception is the foundation of reasoning and actions. It starts with low-level model-free deep-learned perceptual modules, which models the unconscious perception activities. A hierarchical model-based graph model is then stacked on top of the low-level modules, forming a structure of high-level explainable intermediate representation. Such high-level presentation accounts for the explicit perceptual activities, which is the root of the higher-level reasoning.

The ability to reason about the hidden states is a key component in our method. The hierarchical structure with explicit representation of the perceived world, our method is capable to deductively and inductively learn the hidden states of the world, filling the gap between the perceived states and the ground truth states. Such deep understanding of the world's states lays the groundwork for generating proper actions.

Actions are the natural reactions based on the perceived and reasoned world. The artificial brain generates compound actions that react based on the situations, controls the muscle actuated body and continually learn complex movements. Decisions are made as to the instructions transmitted to the rest of the body.

Artificial Nerve

Our artificial nerve system contains a complex structure of functional point cloud. It carries sensory signals to and from the various parts of the body. It helps our artificial brain to manage the unintentional muscles over the body to produce the realistic micro motions.

show more

The functioning of biological nerve system helps the brain to control muscle reflexes and joint motion. Combined with artificial intelligence learning algorithms such as computational neural networks, virtual animals can further adjust and improve the nerve system, and 'learn' to control their intentional and unintentional muscles for various goals. This facilitates realistic motor control process from a high level central brain to low level locomotion behaviors such as balancing, walking, running and swimming.

Muscle Actuated

Our artificial life contains a structure of rigid body with dynamic muscle actuated model. Bones form the shape and support the body. Joints allow skeleton to move flexibly. Muscles are elastic tissues that pull the bones when move.

show more

We incorporate biomechanical constraints in the model to guarantee the authenticity and smooth the torque patterns. More specifically, we model the physiological properties of contractile muscle tissues, and the biomechanical process that leads to changes in activation state.

Our skeleton and muscles have a two-way interaction. Muscles execute forces on the skeleton to change its pose, while the skeleton pose fully determines muscle length, which then influences the contraction dynamics. The route of a muscle is determined by the locations where it attached to the bones, the bony landmarks around which the muscle wraps, and the time varying pose of the joints that the muscle spans.
